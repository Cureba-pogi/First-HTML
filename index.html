Concepts of Rock Cycle and Volcanic Eruption


Volcanic eruption


The concept behind the volcanic eruption is that the Earths core is highly contained with small amounts.
of dissolved gases such as water vapor, carbon dioxide and sulfur which mostly contains oxygen and silicon which is a high temperature molten rocks.



Due to the tectonic plate movements, which is divergent and convergent.
It creates a scenario where two plates collides and splits, making a structure that is suitable for molten rocks to flow through on the Earths crust.
Convergent moves the two tectonics plates collide at 1 another and divergent in the other hand breaks the 1 plate by splitting it, making it into 2 plates
which both plate tectonics leads to molten rocks to flow through.

Once the molten rocks squizes into the gap that the divergent and convergent plate tectonics made,
it squizes within the main vent and some of the molten rocks may tend to leak out of the secondary vent.



Once the molten rocks touches the surface of the Earth, It is now called a "lava" and when it solidifies due to the exposure of the atmosphere,
it is now called an "Igneous rock" and that's where the rock cycle starts.
